I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Information, Risk and Operation Management Department at Mccombs School of Business at the University of Texas at Austin. My research interest is fairness and interpretability in Machine Learning (ML) Systems and Human-AI interaction. As part of my work, I characterize how bias may creep into data labels for training a supervised learning system, and how such label bias may lead to unwanted consequences without awareness. Moreover, interpretable ML are typically being used to enhance effective collaboration between human end users and AI system. In my research, I aim to understand the limits and risks of using ML systems to assist decision-making, and to develop method to mitigate the risks from both ML designers behavioral perspective and algorithm design perspective.
